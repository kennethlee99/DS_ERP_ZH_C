Supervisor Delayed Production Triage

English | 中文

Use this page when released work is missing, delayed, blocked, or unclear during a shift.

Triage Order

  1. Start with Queue System.
  2. Compare with Production Orders.
  3. Confirm the schedule in Planning.
  4. Check quality status in Inspection Records and NCR.
  5. Open Dashboards only for high-level signals; OEE definitions still need business-owner confirmation.

Delay Decision Table

Use this table after collecting the visible evidence. It is written for safe routing: do not use unconfirmed icons, unclear dashboard metrics, or guessed queue states as the only reason for action.

Delayed Work Evidence

When work is late or appears stuck, collect these visible fields before deciding who owns the issue.

EvidenceWhere to look firstWhy it matters
Due time or production dateProduction Orders, PlanningConfirms whether the job is actually late for the current review window.
Planned start/end or planned areaPlanningShows the expected machine, group, line, or date.
Queue stateQueue SystemMap the visible row or missing-row condition to Ready, Active, Paused / blocked, Complete, or Hidden / missing.
Last visible updateQueue System, Production OrdersHelps separate a stale queue row from a newly changed job.
Output versus targetProduction Orders or assigned reporting screenShows whether delay is caused by quantity still open.
Downtime or stop reasonAssigned reporting screen or supervisor noteHelps route the issue to production, engineering, or maintenance.
Owner or current roleProduction Orders, Queue System, Users and RolesShows who can take the next visible action.

What To Record For Handover

  • WO or queue row.
  • Machine/work area.
  • Current visible status.
  • The screen where the issue was found.
  • Screenshot when a button, icon, or status is unclear.
  • Person or role escalated to.
