Operator Run The Next Job Walkthrough

English | 中文

Use this walkthrough when an operator needs to understand what to run next and how to confirm the work is complete enough to hand over.

Goal

Find the next job, confirm it is ready, start through the visible local workflow, report progress through the assigned reporting screen, complete required inspection, and verify the result was saved.

Walkthrough

StepOpenDo thisCheck before moving on
1Queue SystemSelect the machine, line, work area, date, or status filter used by the demo.The row belongs to your machine/work area and matches the part/process you were assigned.
2Queue SystemChoose the first row only when the demo owner or supervisor has confirmed the dispatch rule.Do not assume priority from row order unless the rule is stated.
3Production OrdersIf the row is unclear, look up the WO and compare part, quantity, due date, and process.The WO status supports shop-floor execution.
4Queue SystemUse the visible row action that opens the job or next work step.If the start action is unlabeled, request a screenshot with the action name.
5Assigned production reporting screenRecord output, downtime, or issue notes only in the screen assigned for this deployment.The save or confirmation signal is visible before leaving the page.
6SMARTQC InspectionIf inspection is required, search using the job, stage, lot, or date shown in the work row.Required measurements are saved and the result is visible.
7Inspection RecordsConfirm the saved inspection record if quality status affects release or handover.The record belongs to the same WO/job and shows the expected result.

How To Choose The Next Job

Do not choose work from row order alone unless the supervisor or demo owner says row order is the dispatch rule.

Selection ruleUse whenWhat to check
Assigned machine or work areaThe operator is responsible for one machine, line, or cell.Queue row machine/work area matches the assignment.
Planned date or shiftThe shift plan is the dispatch rule.Row date or planning period matches the current shift.
Supervisor instructionMultiple ready jobs are visible.Supervisor names the WO, part, process, or machine to run.
Quality hold clearedWork was blocked by inspection or NCR.Inspection Records or NCR shows the issue is no longer blocking the job.
Demo script orderThe internal demo has a fixed scenario.Use the WO or part number from the demo script, not row position alone.

Completion Signals

SignalWhat it means
Queue row no longer appears in the ready listThe job may have moved to another state or filter. Confirm with supervisor before assuming completion.
WO or queue status shows a finished stateThe work may be complete for the current step. Match it to the WO and process.
Inspection result is savedQuality entry is recorded, but production completion still depends on the WO or queue status.
Save is disabledA required field may be empty, or the role may not allow the action. Check visible required fields first, then escalate.
